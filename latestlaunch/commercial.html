Commercial Property

Commercial property is an increasingly attractive investment option for Singaporeans, as rising residential prices and cooling measures have redirected investor interest toward commercial real estate.

Zoning

Commercial properties fall under non-residential use categories with specific approved purposes. Office spaces, retail shops in shopping malls, shophouses and shopflats are all considered commercial property. Different zoning classifications exist, including commercial-only, residential with commercial ground floors, and mixed-use properties.

Leases and Restrictions

Commercial leases vary significantly, ranging from 30 years to freehold arrangements. Leases often contain specific conditions—for example, medical centres may restrict business types—and some require third-party consent before transactions can proceed.

Who Can Buy Commercial Properties

Unlike residential properties with numerous eligibility restrictions, commercial properties generally have no restrictions on eligible purchasers. However, mixed-use or residential-component properties may have limitations due to their residential elements.

Loan and Financing

Commercial properties have a loan-to-valuation limit of 80%, compared to 75% for residential properties. Individual borrowers face a 55% Total Debt Servicing Ratio cap.

Stamp Duties

Buyer's Stamp Duty follows a tiered system: 1% on the first $180,000, 2% on the next $180,000, and 3% thereafter. No Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty applies when purchasing multiple commercial properties without residential components.

Property Tax

Commercial property tax is fixed at 10% of Annual Value (determined by IRAS). Mixed-use properties have taxes calculated separately for each component.

Income Tax and GST

Rental income from commercial properties remains subject to income tax. GST applies if the seller is registered, with the material date being completion day.
