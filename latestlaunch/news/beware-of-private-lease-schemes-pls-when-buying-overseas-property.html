Beware Of Private Lease Schemes (PLS) When Buying Overseas Property

12/24/2024

Following Singapore's April 2023 ABSD increase, more Singaporeans are exploring overseas properties, with Johor Bahru attracting top interest. Over 100 Singaporean property buyers faced legal disputes after discovering they unknowingly purchased under Private Lease Scheme (PLS) arrangements rather than gaining full ownership.

The Private Lease Scheme allows landowners to lease freehold land for 30-99 years through a Certificate of Private Lease. Under PLS, buyers function as renters rather than owners, and must surrender properties to developers upon lease expiration.

Reselling PLS properties proves challenging. While initial buyer financing reaches 90%, secondary market sales face bank hesitancy since technically the buyer lacks ownership. Some developers mandate resales back to them at predetermined prices, restricting open-market transactions.

England experienced similar leasehold controversies, prompting regulatory action. Developer-appointed lawyers typically represent the developer, not buyers, leaving purchasers uninformed about PLS drawbacks and escalating ground rent possibilities.

Singapore's HDB differs fundamentally from overseas PLS schemes in two respects: HDB allows financing flexibility, and government backing ensures stability unlike private overseas developers risking insolvency.

Kiwi Lim warned that overseas PLS buyers may lack proprietary rights, potentially facing property confiscation while remaining legally obligated to service housing loans if developers fail.
