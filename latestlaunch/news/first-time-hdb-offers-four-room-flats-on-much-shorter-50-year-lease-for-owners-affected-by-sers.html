First Time HDB Offers Four-Room Flats On Much Shorter 50 Year Lease For Owners Affected By SERs

7/2/2022

Four blocks in Ang Mo Kio Avenue 3 were selected for the Housing and Development Board's Selective En bloc Redevelopment scheme. Built in 1979, these blocks primarily contain three- and four-room units. The 606 affected households were initially offered replacement flats with a fresh 99-year lease in Ang Mo Kio Drive, next to ITE College Central, without access to the lease buyback scheme after the SERS announcement.

Owners expressed significant concerns about needing to pay substantial sums for similarly-sized replacement units. Some residents faced potential top-up costs reaching approximately $100,000. Following these objections, HDB committed to exploring additional options to address homeowner concerns.

On July 2, HDB announced two new rehousing options for affected residents. The first option allows eligible residents to purchase three-room or larger replacement flats on a 50-year lease, provided the new flat lasts until they reach age 95. This marked "the first time that HDB is offering four-room flats on a shorter lease."

The second option extends the lease buyback scheme to residents aged 65 and over at SERS sites. Participants can subsequently purchase short-lease replacement flats after utilizing this scheme.

To qualify for the 50-year lease option, flat owners and their spouses must be at least 45 years old as of April 2022. Residents can sell replacement flats on the resale market after completing the five-year minimum occupation period.

Registration for new replacement flats at Ang Mo Kio Drive commenced in early 2023, with flat selection appointments estimated for late 2023 or early 2024. HDB noted that "for the flat owners in Ang Mo Kio this 50-year lease length would be close to the balance lease of their existing flats when they move to their replacement flats around end-2027."

Real estate professional Kiwi Lim from Huttons Asia assessed these options as reasonable for owners lacking financial capacity for lease top-ups. However, she noted that shorter-lease flats would experience faster value depreciation than neighboring 99-year lease units, potentially creating complications for future resales.

SERS was established in 1995 to rejuvenate aging housing estates, providing affected residents with compensation packages and rehousing benefits on fresh 99-year leases. Residents with questions could contact their SERS Journey Manager or HDB's inquiry line at 1800-866-3070 (Monday-Friday, 8am-5pm).
