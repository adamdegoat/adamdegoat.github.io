New Heights: City Vue @ Henderson Set Record-Breaking $1.728M HDB Resale Price

5/3/2026

The Singapore public housing market has reached a staggering new milestone as a five-room HDB flat at City Vue @ Henderson recently sold for $1.728 million. This transaction sets a new national record for the highest resale price ever paid for an HDB unit, surpassing the previous record of $1.588 million set just months prior.

The record-setting unit is located at 96A Henderson Road, situated on a high-floor spanning the 46th to 48th levels. Several factors contributed to this historic price point: Prime Location in Bukit Merah with proximity to the city center and future Greater Southern Waterfront; Massive Floor Space of approximately 1,216 sq ft, achieving roughly $1,421 per square foot; Remaining Lease of roughly 92 years (completed in 2018); Views and Design featuring modern "DBSS-like" architecture and panoramic vistas.

This sale is not an isolated event but rather the peak of a broader trend. The number of million-dollar HDB flats has seen a significant surge in 2026: Volume Increase with over 100 million-dollar resale transactions in the first few months; Spreading Across Estates with high-value deals appearing in non-mature estates as well.

The $1.728 million tag reinforces the growing price gap between typical resale flats and premium units in choice locations. For many Singaporeans, this sale highlights the continued resilience of the HDB resale market despite various cooling measures.

A record high of 1,594 HDB resale flats were transacted for at least $1 million in 2025. This represented a significant increase of approximately 54% compared to the 1,035 units sold in 2024, with these transactions making up about 6% to 6.9% of the total resale volume in 2025.

Kiwi Lim notes that while million-dollar flats capture headlines, they represent less than 10% of the total HDB resale market, with most transactions occurring at much lower price points, though overall upward pressure on HDB valuations remains a concern for first-time homebuyers.

While $1.7M appears astronomical for an HDB flat, the buyer likely viewed it as a bargain compared to private alternatives. A comparable 1,200 sq ft private condo in the Bukit Merah/Tiong Bahru area would command $2.5M to $3M+. At $1,421 psf, this HDB provides substantial lifestyle value at significant discount to $2,500+ psf private new launch rates.

This record is likely driven by 'right-sizers'—owners selling private properties to move into premium HDBs. Many buyers come from luxury condo or landed property sales. After wait-out periods or age eligibility, they enter the HDB market with cash reserves, paying high Cash-Over-Valuation without hesitation. They seek lifestyle 'trophy homes' with low maintenance fees rather than affordable housing.

City Vue @ Henderson creates a 'mini-monopoly' in the resale market as one of Singapore's tallest HDB developments. Units on the 40th floor and above offer views unreplicable by 99% of other HDB blocks. With lease starting in 2018, the 92-year balance removes lease-decay anxiety plaguing high-value older flats in Queenstown or Tiong Bahru.
