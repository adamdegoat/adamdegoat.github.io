72% Sold On Launch Day: Vela Bay Proves Property Market Is Still Strong

4/25/2026

Kiwi Lim, Associate Group Director of PropNex Realty, reported tracking the Vela Bay launch and noted that results should alert those anticipating market cooling.

By 6 PM on April 25, 2026, Vela Bay—a joint development by SingHaiyi and Chuan Capital—had sold 371 units, representing a 72% absorption rate of the 515-unit project at an average price of $2,886 per square foot.

A Singaporean buyer purchased a five-bedroom penthouse with 180-degree sea views for $5.83 million ($3,303 per square foot), demonstrating strong demand for premium trophy assets.

The Bayshore precinct emerged as a notable market segment, with over half of Vela Bay's units offering sea views that function as effective property value protection vehicles. More than 90% of purchasers were Singaporean.

The project's strong performance represents a significant moment for 2026, with buyers prioritizing rare sea view units despite cautious global economic conditions and establishing new pricing benchmarks for 99-year leasehold outside-central-region properties.

Ground-level observations revealed that entry-level one-bedroom-plus-study units (from $1.2M) sold rapidly, approximately 75% of three-bedroom units (from $2.2M) were absorbed, and local capital dominated purchasing decisions.

Lim characterized the 72% absorption rate as demonstrating buyer confidence, noting that investors are returning to fundamental property value drivers: scarcity, sea views, and strategic connectivity.
