Law Presumes Upper Floor To Be Liable For Leakage And Repairs Unless Proven Otherwise For Condos

11/27/2023

Homeowners of old condos may experience inter-floor water leakage issues as their floor waterproofing wears out. Living in a condominium offers numerous advantages, but the challenge of resolving inter-floor leakage can cause much distress for affected home owners. Whether you've noticed water stains on your ceiling or suspect leaks from a neighboring unit, taking proactive steps to address inter-floor leakage is crucial.

Practical tips on handling inter-floor leakage issues include identifying the source through thorough inspection and collaboration with neighbors. Immediate measures should include placing containers under visible leaks and moving valuables away from affected areas.

Property owners should notify condo management and neighbors immediately, engage professional inspections by qualified plumbers or building inspectors, and cooperate with management to share findings and coordinate repairs.

Additional solutions include investing in professional waterproofing services, undertaking necessary repairs promptly, coordinating with neighbors on synchronized efforts, regularly inspecting units, and seeking legal advice through Singapore's Building Maintenance and Strata Management Act if needed.

Under Singapore's BMSMA, "the upper floor is presumed liable for the leakage and thus the repairs unless he can prove to the contrary." Both units must jointly investigate and proceed with repairs, involving the Management Corporation when necessary.

The Strata Titles Board serves as a forum for resolving disputes among strata-titled property owners, addressing maintenance fees, management, and violations. The STB encourages mediation before formal adjudication, with decisions appealable to the High Court on points of law.

Kiwi Lim advises that "resolving inter-floor leakage issues may require patience and cooperation between many parties." Lim emphasizes that following procedures, seeking professional advice, and maintaining constructive dialogue helps owners find solutions.

Addressing leakage requires collaborative approaches involving residents, management, and professionals. Regular maintenance and proactive measures help prevent future incidents for a healthier living environment.
