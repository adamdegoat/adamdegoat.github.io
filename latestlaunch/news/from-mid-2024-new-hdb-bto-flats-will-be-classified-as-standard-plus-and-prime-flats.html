From Mid-2024, New HDB BTO Flats Will Be Classified As: Standard, Plus and Prime Flats

8/20/2023

At the National Day Rally on August 20, 2023, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong introduced a fresh classification framework for HDB's Build-To-Order projects to reflect their locational characteristics. This enables homebuyers to access more affordable BTO options in desirable areas, as the distinction between newer and mature estates has diminished over time.

Non-mature estates have evolved significantly with improved amenities and transit infrastructure comparable to established estates. HDB will prioritize developing projects within or adjacent to existing estates and central locations due to limited undeveloped land. These strategically positioned sites attract buyers through superior connectivity and amenity proximity.

Three categories will emerge from mid-2024: Standard flats continue baseline subsidies and restrictions; Plus flats occupy choice locations (near MRT stations, town centers) with enhanced subsidies and stricter conditions; Prime flats represent the choicest locations with maximum subsidies and tightest restrictions.

Additional subsidies for Plus flats maintain affordability across income groups. Owners must repay a resale price proportion reflecting extra subsidies upon sale, preventing excessive windfall gains while maintaining equity among homebuyers.

Plus flat owners face a 10-year Minimum Occupation Period before selling or investing in private property. Stricter resale regulations moderate demand and pricing to sustain affordability and social diversity long-term.

The first Plus flats, launching in late 2024, require five to six years construction plus ten years minimum occupancy—meaning earliest resale access around 2039.

These measures apply only prospectively; existing flat owners and current resale markets remain unaffected. HDB will monitor impacts and adjust policies as needed.

Real estate professional Kiwi Lim suggests earlier-launched desirable flats like The Pinnacle @ Duxton and Skyville developments may command premium resale prices due to freedom from stricter restrictions and income ceilings.

Singles eligibility expanded: first-time buyers aged 35+ can now apply for 2-room Flexi flats across Standard, Plus, and Prime projects. They may purchase Standard or Plus units of any size in resale markets or Prime 2-room flats.

Enhancement initiatives for seniors include expanded senior-friendly features through EASE 2.0 programming, precinct upgrades promoting active aging, safer commuting, and new Community Care Apartments integrating customizable services.

The government reaffirmed four foundational principles: home ownership accessibility, inclusivity reflecting societal diversity, fairness prioritizing occupation over speculation, and sustainability ensuring affordability across generations.
