Will Johor Become 'District 29' For Singaporeans in 2026?

1/11/2024

Singapore and Malaysia's Prime Ministers met on January 11, 2024 to celebrate 65% completion of the Johor Bahru-Singapore RTS Link construction, designed to alleviate congestion at the Causeway, one of the world's busiest border crossings.

The rapid transit system will connect Woodlands North station in Singapore to Bukit Chagar station in Johor Bahru, completing journeys in approximately five minutes while serving an estimated 10,000 passengers per hour in each direction.

Co-located customs, immigration, and quarantine facilities mean passengers will clear immigration only once at their departure point, streamlining the cross-border experience.

The project costs RM10 billion (US$2.15 billion), with Singapore contributing 61 percent. Completion is anticipated by late 2026 based on current construction progress.

The connection offers enhanced transportation, reduced road congestion, time savings, economic growth stimulation, environmental benefits through reduced vehicle emissions, strengthened diplomatic ties, and integrated transport system development.

Johor's lower cost of living compared to Singapore may attract residents, though quality of life considerations including safety, amenities, education, and employment opportunities remain important factors in relocation decisions.

Real estate consultant Kiwi Lim suggests the RTS Link may function as a 'cooling measure' for Singapore's property market by making commuting between locations more convenient.
