New Project Sales Process

Buying a new launch follows a set sequence, from registering your interest to selecting a unit on booking day and paying down the price as the project is built. Here's how it actually unfolds.

1. Register your interest

Sign up with the appointed marketing agent (that's me) to receive the e-brochure, floor plans, indicative pricing and your priority access. There is no cost and no obligation to register.

2. VVIP preview

Before public launch, registered buyers are invited to a preview of the showflat. You see the actual layouts, the full price list and unit availability, and you get to plan your preferred stack, floor and unit type ahead of the crowd.

3. e-Application and balloting

If you want to proceed, you submit an expression of interest (e-Application) indicating your preferred units. If the project is oversubscribed, a ballot decides the queue order for booking day. This keeps the process fair and transparent.

4. Booking day

Following your queue number, you select your unit and pay the 5% booking fee. The developer issues you the Option to Purchase (OTP). At this point your unit is reserved.

5. Sale & Purchase Agreement and stamp duty

The developer delivers the Sale & Purchase Agreement (S&P) within about two weeks. You exercise the option by signing the S&P, typically within three weeks, and pay the balance of the downpayment to bring you to 20%.

Stamp duty is due within 14 days of exercising — Buyer's Stamp Duty for everyone, plus Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty if it applies to you. Your loan and CPF are arranged in parallel.

6. Progressive payments

From there you pay the rest of the price in stages tied to construction milestones, under the Progressive Payment Scheme, all the way to completion. See the Progressive Payment guide for the full schedule.

It sounds like a lot, but I guide you through every step. Register your interest and I'll make sure you're ready for booking day.
