Resale HDB Procedures

This comprehensive guide explains the HDB (Housing and Development Board) resale process in Singapore, detailing procedures for both sellers and buyers, timelines, financial considerations, and required documentation.

Updated HDB Resale Process After 1 January 2018

The resale transaction typically requires approximately 8 weeks to complete from application registration. The process involves registering an Intent to Sell through the HDB Resale Portal, where sellers can confirm eligibility, view EIP/SPR quotas, check upgrading status, and review recent transacted prices for comparable properties.

Key Seller Considerations

Sellers should arrange alternative housing before listing to avoid homelessness between transactions.

Reference the HDB resale flat prices portal to determine competitive pricing based on neighborhood market conditions and recent transactions.

The net proceeds equal the purchase price minus outstanding housing loans, CPF refunds with accrued interest, buyer deposits (capped at $5,000), resale levies, upgrading costs, and other outstanding payments including service charges and property taxes.

Buyers may request proof of ownership through MyHDBPage printouts, property tax statements, town council statements, or title deeds. Confirmation letters from HDB branches document ownership and minimum occupation period compliance.

Register Intent to Sell Process

Sellers must personally register through the HDB Resale Portal using Singpass, even when engaging a salesperson. The registration is valid for 12 months and must remain valid when granting the Option to Purchase. Information provided determines eligibility but doesn't constitute final approval until the resale application is processed.

After registration, sellers download the OTP template and must observe a mandatory 7-day cooling-off period before granting it to buyers.

Option to Purchase (OTP) Details

Once agreement is reached, sellers grant the OTP with a maximum option fee of $1,000. Buyers then have 21 calendar days to decide whether to proceed. If exercising the option, buyers pay an additional fee; combined option fees cannot exceed $5,000. During the 21-day period, sellers cannot grant OTPs to other buyers.

Computing Estimated Sale Proceeds

Financial planning tools help sellers calculate expected cash proceeds. The sale proceeds calculator accounts for outstanding mortgages, CPF refunds with interest, buyer deposits, resale levies, and upgrading costs.

Outstanding mortgage loans are deducted from resale price if sufficient; otherwise sellers pay the shortfall in cash. Sellers can check loan balances through MyHDBPage or by contacting their lending bank.

All CPF monies used for the original purchase, plus accrued interest, must be refunded to the CPF Ordinary Account upon sale. Amounts are deducted from proceeds; shortfalls are covered by buyer deposits.

Resale levy is required for second subsidized flat purchases or certain Executive Condo transactions. Sellers can identify levy requirements when registering Intent to Sell.

Upgrading costs are payable by flat owners on the bill issuance date. Outstanding amounts can be deducted from resale price if sufficient, otherwise paid through cash or CPF savings. Status and billing dates are viewable in the Intent to Sell registration.

Request for Value by Buyer

Buyers submitting housing loan applications or using CPF monies must request HDB value confirmation after receiving the OTP. This request must be submitted by the next working day following the option grant date. A $120 processing fee applies (GST inclusive).

Required submission information includes property address, OTP details, scanned OTP page 1, seller information, all buyer names and identification numbers, and requestor contact details.

OTP Documentation Requirements

The OTP serial number must be identical across all pages. Both parties fill pages 1, 2, and 4, countersigning page bottoms. Sellers complete page 8 but leave the acceptance section blank. Buyers sign the acceptance section within 21 days if exercising their purchase option.

On page 4, buyers and sellers agree on resale application submission timelines—typically 15, 30, or 60 days—based on individual selling and moving needs. Buyers submit applications first; sellers follow within 7 days after buyer submission.

Resale Application Submission

Agents and individuals submit applications through the online portal, with documents indicated during submission. All required documents must be uploaded or applications are rejected. Seller contact information ensures SMS/email updates on transaction progress.

Checking Ethnic and SPR Quotas

Both ethnic integration policy (EIP) and Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR) quotas are revised monthly on the first day. Buyers and sellers should verify eligibility through the HDB Ethnic Integration Policy map before issuing OTPs. The online map allows users to enter block or postal code and ethnic details to confirm quota availability.

Special Purchasing Schemes

HDB Contra Cases involve transactions where sellers purchase new flats before completing resale transactions, requiring specific procedural accommodations.

The Two Adjoining Flats Scheme allows qualified buyers to purchase adjacent flats under modified procedures and eligibility requirements.
