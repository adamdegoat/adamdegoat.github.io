Mortgage & Home Loan Glossary

Useful Information for Home Buyers & Owners.

Glossary of Terms

Accrued Interest: Interest that has been incurred but not yet paid.

Annual Value: The estimated annual rent of a property, excluding furniture and fittings. Determined by the Income Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) through analysis of comparable property rents.

Approval-In-Principle (AIP): An approved loan amount from the Bank based on credit reports and borrower affordability. Not binding; subject to property valuation. Typically valid for 30 days.

Amortization: Gradual reduction of loan amount through regular payments over a predetermined period, covering both principal and interest.

Borrower: The person(s) taking the home loan from the Bank. All property owners pledged as mortgage are borrowers.

Bridging Loan: Short-term financing for down-payment (up to 10% of valuation limit) before proceeds from existing property sale are received. Can also cover stamp duty and legal fees.

Cancellation Fee: Fee payable when a loan is cancelled before disbursement.

Cash-back: Illegal arrangements where buyers, sellers, and agents increase declared purchase prices to enable higher loans or CPF withdrawals. Creates larger monthly installments and higher fees.

Caveat: A notice of claim on property interest, lodged with the Singapore Land Authority after deposit payment to establish priority of claims.

Certificate of Statutory Completion (CSC) / Temporary Occupancy Permit (TOP): Issued by the Commissioner of Building Control when building works are complete. CSC is typically issued one year after TOP.

Collateral: The property secured against a home loan.

Completion of Sale and Purchase: When the seller/developer conveys legal title of the property to the buyer.

Construction Loan: Short-term financing for new property construction, reconstruction, or alterations to existing property.

Conversion: Transferring an existing loan package to another within the same Bank.

CPF Housing Withdrawal Limit: Two types, Valuation Limit (VL), the lower of purchase price or property value; and Withdrawal Limit (WL), maximum CPF usage beyond VL, set at 120% of VL.

Credit Report: Information from Credit Bureau (Singapore) Pte Ltd showing a borrower's credit payment history, used by Banks to assess creditworthiness.

Default: Failure to meet loan requirements or repay monthly installments on time for several months, potentially leading to property repossession.

Delinquent: Failure to make payments by the due date, which may trigger a Notice of Default.

Down-payment: Upfront lump sum payment made at property purchase.

Early Redemption: Repayment of loan (principal and interest) before maturity, either partially or fully.

Equity Loan: Second loan on existing private property that has appreciated in value, with no usage restrictions. CPF cannot be used for repayment.

Fire Insurance: Provides coverage for loss and damages caused by fire. Compulsory for HDB flat owners; advisable for private property owners.

Guarantor: A person who agrees to be responsible for another person's mortgage in case of default.

Home Insurance / Mortgage Reducing Term Assurance (MRTA): Protects the borrower's family from losing the home if the borrower becomes disabled or passes away. Compulsory for HDB flat owners using CPF.

Home Loan / Mortgage Loan: Loan secured by the borrower's property. Non-repayment can result in property repossession.

Interest Rate: Rate charged on the loan. Can be fixed, variable, or a combination. Variable rates typically benchmark against SIBOR.

Joint Tenancy: Ownership where all owners have equal interest regardless of contribution amount. Property passes to surviving co-owners upon death.

Late Charge: Fee payable when a monthly instalment is not received by the due date.

Legal Fee / Conveyance Fee: Fee for a lawyer's legal paperwork services for property purchase and home loan. Can be paid with CPF funds.

Letter of Offer (LO) / Facility Letter: Contract from the Bank stating loan package terms. Signed by the borrower to bind both parties.

Loan Amount: Total amount the borrower agrees to repay to the Bank according to loan agreement terms.

Loan Tenor: Number of years planned to repay the loan, usually up to 35 years.

Loan-to-Value (LTV): Amount borrowable as a percentage of current market valuation or adjusted purchase price (whichever is lower). Up to 80% if no outstanding loans on other residential properties.

Lock-in Period: Predetermined years when the borrower is penalised for changing contract terms through cancellation, prepayment, or conversion.

Mortgagee: The lender (the Bank).

Mortgagor: The owner(s) of the pledged property. All owners must be loan borrowers.

Option to Purchase: Right to buy property at a specified price within a specified validity period. Requires a booking/option fee, forfeited if not exercised in time.

Prime Lending Rate / Board Rate: Interest rate charged to the Bank's most creditworthy customers, typically used as a loan benchmark.

Property Tax: Annual government tax computed as a percentage of annual property value. 4% if owner-occupied; 10% if rented out.

Refinancing: Applying for a new loan from a different bank to replace an existing loan, typically to lower monthly repayments through better rates.

Repricing: Similar to refinancing but with the same bank, requesting a rate reduction.

Sale & Purchase Agreement: Private contract between seller and buyer for property sale and purchase.

Singapore Interbank Offer Rate (SIBOR): Rate at which Singapore financial institutions lend/borrow unsecured funds to each other. Loans can be based on 1-month or 3-month SIBOR, repriced accordingly.

Stamp Duty: Government tax validating legal documents and financial contracts. Payable by property buyers. Two types: Purchase Stamp Duty and Mortgage Stamp Duty.

Swap Offer Rate (SOR): SIBOR plus lending costs incurred by banks. A transparent rate reflecting market conditions, repriced every 3 months if loan-based.

Tenancy-in-Common: Ownership where a deceased owner's property interest becomes part of their estate, unlike joint tenancy where it passes automatically to surviving co-owners.

Title Deed: Document evidencing a person's legal right or title to the property.

Valuation Fee: Fee for determining property market value, required by banks to calculate the maximum loan amount.
