Parquet Timber Flooring, Polishing & Restoration

Parquet flooring offers a distinctive aesthetic appeal. Each tile truly is unique, as materials are combined and then cut before installation. You can customize patterns, materials, grains, and coloring, making each room distinctive. Being wood-based, parquet provides warmth to any space.

Parquet flooring offers a distinctive aesthetic appeal. Each tile truly is unique, as materials are combined and then cut before installation. You can customize patterns, materials, grains, and coloring, making each room distinctive. Being wood-based, parquet provides warmth to any space.

However, wood parquetry is vulnerable to scratches from sharp objects. It's not advised to wear high heels on parquet flooring, and to use doormats or rugs at entry points. Regular maintenance through resealing, sanding, and polishing helps preserve appearance and extends lifespan.

Maintenance Tips for Parquet Timber Flooring

Avoid exposing parquet to excessive moisture; place floor mats near bathrooms.

Dry surfaces after mopping.

Ventilate rooms to prevent humidity buildup affecting the floor.

Use soft paddings or rubber soles on furniture legs to prevent scratches.

Common Parquet Issues & Solutions

Lacklustre Varnished Surfaces: Wear and tear dulls varnish finishes. Solution: re-varnish the floor.

Localised Discolouration: Prolonged water absorption or sunlight exposure cause discoloration. Solutions include identifying water sources, using window shades, and re-varnishing if needed.

Open Gaps Between Timber Strips: Dry environments cause wood shrinkage. Solutions: seal gaps with matching putty and re-varnish as necessary.

Hollow or Bulged Parquet Strips: Water seepage underneath causes adhesion loss. Solutions: prevent water contact and replace affected strips.
