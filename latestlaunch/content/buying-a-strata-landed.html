Buying a Strata Landed (Cluster House)

Cluster houses offer prospective homebuyers an appealing hybrid option: they combine the appeal of landed properties with condominium-style amenities and facilities like swimming pools, gyms, maintained gardens, and 24-hour security.

What is a Cluster House?

A cluster development comprises terraces, semi-detached homes, bungalows, or a combination thereof, all sharing facilities within the development. These properties carry strata titles rather than conventional land ownership.

The Crucial Difference: Strata vs. Traditional Landed

With conventional bungalows, owners hold title to the underlying land. With cluster bungalows under strata ownership, buyers receive a share of the whole estate — meaning you have joint ownership of the land as well as of the common areas like walkways and gardens.

Strata-titled properties are priced based on built-in floor area, whereas conventional landed properties use per-square-foot land valuations with premiums for structures.

Financial Considerations

Monthly Maintenance Fees: Owners must pay ongoing fees for upkeep. Hillcrest Villa residents pay approximately $200 monthly, while Illoura residents pay $1,400–$1,500 as costs are distributed among just 30 units.

Shared Upgrade Costs: Cluster home owners bear proportionally larger expenses for common area improvements than apartment dwellers, despite potentially not using all facilities.

Ownership Restrictions

Unlike conventional landed home owners, cluster house proprietors cannot freely rebuild properties or alter external facades without management committee approval.

Market Appeal and Rental Yields

Newer projects command stronger rental performance. Properties in Hillcrest Villa and The Teneriffe generate $11,000–$13,000 monthly rentals, while older developments like Binjai Crest fetch approximately $8,000. Rental yields reach approximately four percent annually.

Advantages: 24-hour security, gated environments, basement parking eliminating street congestion, new construction avoiding renovation expenses, and contemporary design appeal to many buyers.
