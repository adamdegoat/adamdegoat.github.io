What Should I Do When There Are Defects in My New Home?

When you purchase a new home in Singapore, you have protections under the Defects Liability Period (DLP). This safeguard ensures the developer remains responsible for addressing construction-related defects discovered within a specified timeframe.

The Defects Liability Period

The standard DLP lasts 1 year, beginning from whichever comes first: the date you receive actual vacant possession (key collection), or the 15th day after receiving the Temporary Occupancy Permit (TOP).

During this period, the developer must cover all repair costs for legitimate defects.

What Counts as a Defect?

A defect typically involves either faulty workmanship/materials or construction that deviates from your sale and purchase agreement specifications. Defects stem from the building process itself, not normal wear and tear.

Steps to Address Defects

Initial Notice: Submit written notice detailing specific defects to the developer. Allow one month for response.

Arrange Inspection: Coordinate with the developer to schedule a joint inspection or arrange repairs.

Prepare the Area: Clear the defect area to facilitate smooth repairs and prevent damage to your belongings.

Grant Access: Ensure someone is present when workers arrive to perform rectification.

Confirm Completion: Acknowledge that repairs are finished to document the work status.

If the Developer Doesn't Respond

After one month without response, issue a Notice of Intent to rectify defects, including estimated costs. If the developer fails to act within 14 days, you may hire contractors yourself and recover costs from the developer's stakeholder funds.

Resolving Disputes

If disagreements arise regarding defects or repair quality, consider these options.

Mediation: An impartial mediator helps both parties reach a mutual agreement. This is typically faster and less costly than litigation.

Other Options: Neutral evaluation, arbitration, or litigation are available if mediation fails.

The Singapore Mediation Centre (SMC) provides professional mediation services staffed by former judges, senior lawyers, and construction industry experts.

Inspection Checklist

Common areas to inspect include structural elements, walls, flooring, plumbing fixtures, electrical systems, and paint quality, though specific concerns depend on your property type.
