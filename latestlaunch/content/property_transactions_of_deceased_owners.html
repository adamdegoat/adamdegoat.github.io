Property Transactions Of Deceased Owners

Upon a property owner's death, the property may be disposed of by administrators or executors of the estate. However, several important considerations must be addressed before any transaction occurs, including questions about property holding arrangements and whether a will exists.

Manner of Holding

When property is held jointly with other owners, the rule of survivorship applies if held as joint tenants, surviving owners automatically become registered proprietors upon filing a Notice of Death with the Singapore Land Authority. Conversely, if held as tenants-in-common, the rule of survivorship does not apply, requiring either a will or letters of administration.

The Deceased Had Drawn Up a Will

Executors named in the will must obtain a Grant of Probate from Singapore courts. Once issued, executors may transfer property to beneficiaries or file a Transmission Application with the Land Authority to become temporary proprietors for property sales, provided the will contains a power of sale.

The Deceased Had NOT Drawn Up a Will

Administrators must obtain Letters of Administration from the courts. The Probate and Administration Act establishes priority classes for administrator appointment: spouse, children, parents, siblings, nephews/nieces, grandparents, and uncles/aunts. Up to four administrators may be appointed, and minors require guardians. Once Letters of Administration are issued, administrators may transfer property or file Transmission Applications for sales.

Section 35(2), Conveyancing and Law of Property Act

Property sales by legal representatives cannot occur "after the expiration of 6 years from his death unless with the sanction of the court," except where powers of sale are explicitly contained in or implied from the will.

Existing Mortgage

When co-owned property has a mortgage, the deceased's death typically triggers a default event. Surviving co-owners usually must refinance rather than have the loan recalled, requiring they meet current banking standards and debt servicing ratios.
