Income Ceiling for Executive Condominiums

To buy an Executive Condo, your household income must fall under a fixed ceiling. This is the main financial eligibility test for ECs, and it sits alongside the other qualifying rules on citizenship, age and household structure.

The $16,000 ceiling

Your average gross monthly household income must not exceed $16,000. This limit was raised from $14,000 in September 2019 and remains in force today.

Gross income means before CPF deductions, and it is the combined income of all the applicants in the household. The income is typically averaged over the 12 months prior to application.

It works alongside the other EC rules

Meeting the income ceiling is necessary but not sufficient. You also need to satisfy the eligibility scheme (for example a family nucleus), citizenship requirements, the minimum age, and the rules on owning or having disposed of other property.

If your income is comfortably under $16,000 you may also qualify for a CPF Housing Grant, see the EC grant guide for the amounts.
