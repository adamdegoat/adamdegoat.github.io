Buying a Property – Use CPF Before Age 55

At age 55, Singaporeans face significant changes to their CPF accounts. The system requires building a Retirement Account with a Minimum Sum of $155,000, plus a Medisave Account minimum of $43,500, totalling $198,500 in required reserves.

Key CPF Mechanics at Age 55

When reaching 55, funds transfer from the Special Account (SA) into a newly created Retirement Account (RA). Up to 50% of the Minimum Sum can be satisfied through property pledges if CPF was used for the purchase. However, selling property after 55 requires returning previously withdrawn CPF amounts plus accrued interest to the CPF account.

Property Pledge Strategy

Property pledges are not automatic. If forgotten at age 55, the account holder can pledge later, though the Minimum Sum may have increased by then. Without pledging a first property, minimal withdrawal capacity exists between ages 55–65.

Multiple Property Purchases

Those owning one CPF-funded property may purchase a second property if they have set aside half the Minimum Sum ($77,500). However, purchasing a second property before 55 carries significant costs: Additional Buyer Stamp Duty of 7%, reduced loan eligibility to 50% LTV, and stricter TDSR requirements due to age-related loan tenure limitations.

Real-World Scenario

For a 54-year-old with $120,000 in the Ordinary Account and $55,000 in the Special Account who owns a pledged property, up to $97,500 remains available for a second property purchase. This amount equals total CPF savings minus the $77,500 minimum sum requirement.
