Progressive Payment For New Condo (BUC) Projects

New condos are sold while still being built (Building Under Construction, or BUC), and you pay for them in stages under the Progressive Payment Scheme. Rather than paying everything upfront, your payments — and your loan drawdown — track the construction milestones.

Why payments are staged

Under the Progressive Payment Scheme, you pay a defined percentage of the price as each construction stage is completed. Your bank disburses the loan in the same steps, so you only pay interest on the amount drawn down so far — which keeps early-year repayments lower than on a completed property.

The standard payment schedule

Upon grant of the Option to Purchase — 5% (booking fee, in cash).

Upon signing the Sale & Purchase Agreement, within 8 weeks of OTP — 15% (bringing you to 20%).

Completion of foundation work — 10%.

Completion of reinforced concrete framework — 10%.

Completion of brick walls — 5%.

Completion of roofing and ceiling — 5%.

Completion of door and window frames, electrical wiring, internal plastering and plumbing — 5%.

Completion of car parks, roads and drains serving the development — 5%.

On Temporary Occupation Permit (TOP), when you collect keys — 25%.

On Certificate of Statutory Completion (CSC) — the final 15%.

Loan-to-Value and your downpayment

For a first housing loan, the maximum Loan-to-Value (LTV) is 75% of the price or valuation. That falls to 55% if the loan tenure runs beyond 30 years or past your 65th birthday.

At 75% LTV, your downpayment is 25%: at least 5% must be paid in cash, and the remaining 20% can come from cash and/or your CPF Ordinary Account. Buyers with existing home loans face lower LTV limits and larger cash requirements.

Plan it properly

The staged structure is buyer-friendly, but the cash-versus-CPF split and the stamp duty due early on still need planning. I can map out the full cash-flow timeline for any launch you're considering — just ask.
