Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) for Residential Property

SSD is payable on all residential properties and residential lands that are bought on or after 20 Feb 2010 and sold within the holding period.

Key Determining Factors

Whether SSD applies depends on: the type of property sold, the date of purchase or acquisition, the date of sale or disposal, and the applicable rates.

A. Type of Property Sold or Disposed

The property must be classified as residential property.

B. Date of Purchase or Acquisition

Residential properties purchased on or after 20 Feb 2010 trigger SSD if sold within the holding period.

For property transferred pursuant to divorce: the acquisition date is the marriage date, or the date of original purchase of the property, whichever is later.

For property transferred pursuant to inheritance: the acquisition date is the date of original purchase by the deceased.

For transfer of HDB flat within family: the acquisition date is the earliest date of acquisition of the flat by any of the existing owners, who continually holds an interest in the flat from the time of acquisition to disposal.

For non-residential property rezoned to residential or with use changed to residential, the acquisition date is the rezoning or use-change date.

C. Date of Sale or Disposal

Date of Sale/Disposal refers to: (1) the date of execution of Option to Purchase by the buyer to the seller's offer to sell; (2) the date of Sale & Purchase Agreement signed by the seller and buyer; or (3) the date of transfer where (1) and (2) are not applicable.

D. Applicable Rates

SSD rates range from 1% to 16% depending on the holding period, with shorter holding periods incurring higher percentages.

SSD is computed by applying the requisite SSD rate on the higher of the selling price or the market value of the property as at the date of sale or disposal.

SSD payable is rounded down to the nearest dollar.

For shophouses with residential portions or HDB shops with living quarters, SSD is payable on the value of the residential component if the property is sold within the holding period.

Where parts were acquired at different times, the holding period for each part acquired will be computed from the respective acquisition date. For partial interest sales, SSD payable will be based on the higher of the selling price or market value of the partial interest.

Exemptions from SSD for Residential Properties

No application to Commissioner of Stamp Duties required for the following cases:

1. Licensed housing developers selling residential properties developed by them.

2. Public authorities (HDB, JTC) exercising functions and duties.

3. Residential property owners when properties are acquired by the Government under the Land Acquisitions Act.

4. Individuals adjudged bankrupt who are required to dispose of residential properties.

5. Companies disposing of residential properties upon involuntary winding up.

6. Foreigners required to sell under the Residential Properties Act.

7. HDB flat sellers/transferors who bought on or after 30 Aug 2010, with flats identified for the Selective Enbloc Redevelopment Scheme (SERS), selling in the open market before HDB claims them.

8. HDB flat sellers/transferors returning flats to HDB via re-possession or SERS.

9. A person owning an HDB flat who inherits another HDB flat and is required under HDB regulations to dispose of either flat (applies to disposals on or after 18 Dec 2015).

10. A person owning a non-HDB flat who inherits an HDB flat and is required under HDB regulations to dispose of the inherited HDB flat (applies to disposals on or after 18 Dec 2015).

11. A person owning an HDB flat who marries a person owning another HDB flat, and is required under HDB regulations to dispose of either flat (applies to disposals on or after 18 Dec 2015).

Remission of SSD for Residential Properties

Application for remission is required. Non-licensed developers may be eligible for remission when selling residential properties developed by them, only if they are registered companies or businesses lawfully carrying on a housing development business.

Administration

Payment is made via the e-Stamping Portal.

Law firms representing sellers are required to fill in the Seller's Stamp Duty for Residential Properties Declaration Form (mandatory).

Completed forms need not be submitted to IRAS, but law firms must retain original declaration forms for at least 5 years from the sale/disposal date, as IRAS may request them during audits.
