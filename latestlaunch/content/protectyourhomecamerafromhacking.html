Prevent Home IP Cameras From Being Hacked

Security cameras in Singapore homes have been compromised, with stolen footage shared on pornographic websites. An organised hacker syndicate exploited vulnerabilities in internet protocol (IP) cameras commonly used for home security and remote monitoring.

The Hacking Incident

According to AsiaOne's October 12, 2020 report, "Footage from '50,000' home cameras hacked and sold on porn sites," a coordinated group of hackers gained unauthorised access to over 50,000 cameras across multiple countries including Singapore, Thailand, South Korea, and Canada.

The stolen videos featured families, children, and individuals in private moments—often in bedrooms, bathrooms, and living spaces. The hacker group operated through Discord and charged membership fees of approximately $203 USD for lifetime access, with premium tiers offering tutorials on exploiting cameras.

How Cameras Get Hacked

Webcams become vulnerable through malware downloaded via phishing links, suspicious email attachments, or compromised websites. Attackers exploit software vulnerabilities to gain remote access without the victim's knowledge.

Seven Prevention Strategies

1. Physical coverage — Cover your camera with tape when not in use, blocking visual access even if remotely controlled.

2. Disconnect external cameras — Unplug desktop webcams when idle to prevent remote access.

3. Update software regularly — Install security patches immediately for video conferencing apps and all software to close vulnerabilities.

4. Download from official sources — Only install apps from legitimate stores and review permission requests before installation.

5. Avoid suspicious links — Don't click links in emails or messages with grammatical errors or unfamiliar senders, as they often distribute malware.

6. Install antivirus with webcam protection — Use security software offering specific camera protection features.

7. Power down when not using — Shut down your computer entirely rather than using sleep mode, preventing malware activation.
