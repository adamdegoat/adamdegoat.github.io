Guides / Decoupling (Fractional Transaction)

Decoupling (Fractional Transaction)

A fractional transaction, or "decoupling," allows property owners to transfer shares in a property to enable one party to exit and purchase another property with reduced or no Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD). The transfer is assessed based on the number of properties owned by each individual.

What is Decoupling?

"Decoupling" refers to registered proprietors removing one name from the title to free themselves from property counting for Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD) and ABSD calculations. This legal mechanism, termed a part sale and purchase of shares, allows the outgoing owner to purchase another residential property without paying ABSD or at a lower rate.

Financing: Private Properties vs HDB Flats

For private properties, decoupling follows MAS and bank financing rules. If there's a CPF charge or outstanding mortgage, the remaining owner must refinance or restructure loans. The purchaser may need a fresh loan to cover the part share purchase.

HDB flats allow ownership transfers only under six scenarios: divorce, marriage, medical reasons, death of owner, financial hardship, or renunciation of Singapore citizenship.

Determining Purchase Price of Part Share

When lenders arrange property valuations for loan restructuring, the part share purchase price can be pegged to market value at contract time. Without a valuation, proprietors risk undervalued transactions or insufficient stamping. Stamp duty is calculated on whichever is higher: purchase price or market value. Insufficient stamping results in penalties up to four times the IRAS-imposed amount.

Transfer by Gift or Nominal Consideration

Section 16 of the Stamp Duties Act mandates that voluntary dispositions inter vivos receive stamp duty "as if it were a conveyance or transfer on sale." Stamp duties apply based on market value of the transferred share, with insufficient payment carrying the same penalties as undervalued sales.

Stamp Duty Application

BSD and possibly ABSD apply to part share purchases, depending on the purchaser's profile.

Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD)

Properties purchased on or after March 11, 2017, and sold within three years incur SSD on the sale price.

Stamp Duty Remission, Transfer of Interest in Property

IRAS recognizes partial ABSD remission when: (1) the instrument transfers partial interest in a single residential property to the transferee(s); (2) the transferee is a relevant individual, a Singapore Citizen owning interests in two residential properties or a Singapore Permanent Resident owning interests in one; and (3) one of those properties is the same property being transferred.
