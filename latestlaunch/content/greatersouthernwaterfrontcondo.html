The Greater Southern Waterfront Condo Projects

The Greater Southern Waterfront comprises 30km of the southern coastline of Singapore. The PSA terminals at Tanjong Pagar, Keppel, and Brani will move out by 2027, redeveloping this prime land for more housing, commercial and entertainment opportunities.

Featured Condo Launches Near GSW

Three new condo projects are highlighted near the Greater Southern Waterfront: One Pearl Bank (D03) — iconic city condo by CapitaLand, 3 mins walk to Outram Park MRT; The Reef at King's Dock (D04) — ultra luxury harbourfront by Mapletree and Keppel Land; The Landmark — iconic project near CBD and Chinatown by ZACD Group and MCC Land.

Development Overview

The Government announced GSW plans on 18 August 2019. Spanning 2,000 hectares, the area is six times the size of Marina Bay and includes PSA city terminals at multiple locations.

Stage One Development

Initial development begins when Keppel Club lease expires around 2021. Approximately 9,000 housing units (public and private) will be constructed. Additional plans include revitalising decommissioned power stations at Pasir Panjang and enhancing Sentosa beaches.

Stage Two Development

When PSA vacates Pulau Brani by 2027, the entire island becomes a recreation destination. Plans include Downtown South development and new attractions. Further development follows Pasir Panjang Port departure in 2040, incorporating offices supporting 'live, work, play' concepts.

Environmental Integration

The entire Greater Southern Waterfront will be linked up to surrounding green areas, such as from West Coast Park to East Coast Park, and connecting the Rail Corridor and Sentosa.

Market Perspective

The development represents a 20–30 year initiative expected to generate similar activity to Marina Bay. Current resale prices in the area show 99-year leasehold homes trading near $3,000 psf, with HDB 4-room units around $900,000.
