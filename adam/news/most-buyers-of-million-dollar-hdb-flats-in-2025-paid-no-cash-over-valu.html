Most Buyers of Million-Dollar HDB Flats in 2025 Paid No Cash Over Valuation2025年大多数百万组屋买家无需支付现金超额估值

When an HDB flat sells above $1 million, many people assume the buyer must be handing over extra cash on top of what the bank values the flat at. That gap is called cash over valuation, or COV. But PropNex data covering 2025 transactions shows that the majority of buyers in the million-dollar HDB resale segment paid no COV at all.

The reason is that bank valuations have caught up with what the market is actually transacting at in this segment. As million-dollar HDB resale deals have become more frequent, valuers have adjusted their benchmarks to reflect the new normal. Buyers are largely financing these purchases through their loan and CPF in the conventional way, without needing to come up with an extra pile of cash outside the formal transaction.

This matters for anyone who has been sitting out the market on the assumption that buying at the top end of the HDB resale range would require a large cash top-up they did not have. The financing picture here is more straightforward than many people expect. It is still worth checking the actual valuation for the specific flat you want before assuming anything, but the data says the old rule of thumb about COV being unavoidable in this price bracket no longer holds.

原因在于，银行估值已经追上了这一价格区间的实际市场成交价。随着百万组屋转售交易变得越来越普遍，估价师也相应调整了基准，以反映新的市场常态。买家大多以常规方式通过贷款和CPF完成这些购买，不需要在正式交易之外额外筹备一大笔现金。

这对那些一直因为担心需要拿出大笔现金补差价而在场外观望的人来说，是个重要信息。实际上，这里的融资情况比很多人预想的要简单得多。在买任何特定组屋之前，仍然值得核实一下实际估值，但数据说明，那条关于这一价格区间COV无可避免的老经验，已经不再适用了。
