HDB Resale Prices Have Fallen for Two Consecutive Quarters: What It Means If You Want to SellHDB转售价格连续两个季度下跌：有意出售的屋主该怎么看

HDB resale prices have now fallen for two consecutive quarters, a clear break from the sustained gains the market delivered in recent years. This is not a freefall, but the direction has shifted and the days of sellers reliably getting more than their asking price are fading.

The cooling measures introduced over the past few years are doing their intended job. Higher Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty rates, tighter loan-to-value limits, and a larger pipeline of BTO flats coming through have all taken pressure off the resale market. Whether this turns out to be a short correction before prices stabilise, or the start of a longer soft patch, depends partly on how much new supply keeps coming and how economic conditions hold up.

For sellers, the practical message is to price realistically from the start. Overpriced units are sitting on the market longer, and buyers now have genuine room to negotiate in a way they did not a year or two ago. Setting expectations correctly matters more than ever.

For buyers, two soft quarters is not a signal to wait indefinitely. The fundamentals that make a flat worth buying, meaning location, flat type and remaining lease, have not changed. If a flat suits your needs and is priced fairly, the current market gives you leverage to negotiate rather than a reason to keep sitting on the sidelines.

HDB转售价格已连续两个季度下跌，与近年来市场持续上涨的走势明显不同。这并非急剧下跌，但方向已经转变，卖家轻松超出要价成交的时代正在消退。

过去几年推出的降温措施正在发挥预期效果。更高的ABSD税率、更严格的贷款比例上限，以及持续增加的BTO供应，都在为转售市场减压。至于这是价格在企稳前的短暂调整，还是较长期疲软的开始，部分取决于新供应的持续情况以及整体经济走势。

对卖家而言，实际的建议是从一开始就定出合理价格。定价过高的单位在市场上挂牌时间越来越长，买家现在确实拥有一两年前所没有的议价空间。正确设定预期，比以往任何时候都更重要。

对买家而言，两个季度的疲软并不意味着可以无限期观望。决定一套组屋是否值得购买的基本因素，地点、房型和剩余租约年限，并没有改变。如果一套组屋符合你的需求且定价合理，当前市场给你的是谈判筹码，而不是继续旁观的理由。
