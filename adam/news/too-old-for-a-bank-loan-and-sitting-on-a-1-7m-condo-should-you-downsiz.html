Too Old for a Bank Loan and Sitting on a $1.7M Condo: Should You Downsize to a Resale HDB?年纪太大申请不到银行贷款，手持一套$1.7M的condo：该换购转售HDB吗？

$1.7M condo value owned outright全款持有的condo价值

Owning a $1.7 million condo outright sounds like a strong position, but once you no longer qualify for a bank loan, your options narrow quickly. Selling the private property and buying a resale HDB flat is one of the cleaner paths to unlocking that equity while still having a home to live in through retirement.

The rules have real teeth here. You can buy a resale HDB flat after selling your private property, but you must use it as your primary residence and cannot hold another private property at the same time. Depending on your citizenship status and whether you have previously owned an HDB flat, the 15-month wait-out period may also apply. On top of that, Buyer's Stamp Duty applies to the resale flat purchase, and CPF withdrawal rules will affect how much of your sale proceeds you can keep liquid.

The numbers can still make strong sense for the right person. Selling at $1.7 million and buying a resale flat for, say, $700,000 to $800,000 frees up a significant cash buffer for retirement. The honest trade-off is lifestyle: you give up condo facilities and the status that comes with private property. But for many near-retirement homeowners, getting that capital working for them matters more than keeping a pool they rarely use.

If this scenario fits your situation, speak to both a financial adviser and a property lawyer before you act. The order in which you sell and buy, and what you do with CPF proceeds, has implications that vary depending on your exact circumstances and age.
