$950M freehold site sale price永久地契地段成交价

The Tan Boon Liat Building on Outram Road has changed hands for $950 million, making it one of the largest freehold commercial transactions Singapore has seen. The building sits on a sizable freehold land parcel in a well-connected part of the city fringe, which explains why it attracted this level of developer interest and this price.

Freehold sites in central Singapore rarely come to market, so deals of this kind attract close attention. The expectation is that the buyer will redevelop the site, most likely into a mixed-use project combining residential units with commercial or retail elements. A residential launch here, if it proceeds, would almost certainly be positioned as a premium product given the address and the land cost paid.

For buyers, the $950 million price tag confirms that developer appetite for well-located freehold land remains firm despite broader market cooling. Any future residential launch on this site is realistically a few years away, but the location and tenure make it a site worth keeping on your radar if you value a central address and freehold status.

Outram Road的Tan Boon Liat Building以$950 million易手，成为新加坡规模最大的永久地契商业地产交易之一。这栋建筑坐落于一块面积可观的永久地契土地上，位处城市边缘交通便利的地段，正是这一点吸引了如此高的发展商兴趣和成交价格。

永久地契地段在新加坡中心区域极少进入市场，因此此类交易向来备受关注。市场预期买家将对该地段进行重新发展，最有可能是打造成结合住宅单位与商业或零售元素的综合用途项目。如果住宅部分得以推出，考虑到所在地址和支付的地价，几乎可以肯定会定位为高端产品。

对于买家而言，$950 million的成交价证实，尽管市场整体处于降温状态，发展商对优质永久地契地段的兴趣依然坚实。该地段任何未来住宅项目的推出，现实来看还需要数年时间，但其地点和地契性质使它值得持续关注，尤其是对重视中心地段和永久地契身份的买家而言。
