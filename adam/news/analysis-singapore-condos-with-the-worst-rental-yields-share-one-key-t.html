Analysis: Singapore Condos With the Worst Rental Yields Share One Key Trait分析：新加坡租金回报最差的condo有一个共同特征

Data on condo rental yields across Singapore shows a consistent pattern among the worst performers. The projects that deliver the thinnest returns tend to share at least one of three problems: too many similar units competing for the same pool of tenants in the same corridor, a purchase price that was too high relative to achievable rents from the start, or a location that simply does not draw strong rental demand.

Oversupply is a quiet killer. A corridor that sees four or five large condo launches within a short period will have landlords competing against each other, which pushes rents down even as purchase prices stay elevated. Pair that with a project that is not within easy walking distance of an MRT station or a major employment centre, and you have the formula for yields that barely cover costs.

The practical takeaway for anyone buying a condo with rental income in mind is to run the numbers before you commit, not after. Take a realistic monthly rent figure for that unit type and location, multiply by 12, and divide by your total purchase cost including stamp duties. If the gross yield comes in below 2.5 percent, the numbers are already thin and any vacancy period or maintenance cost will eat whatever is left. Knowing what drags yield down gives you a checklist to work through before you sign.

新加坡各地condo租金回报率数据显示，表现最差的项目有一个规律性模式。回报最薄的项目，往往至少存在以下三个问题之一：同一地带有太多相似单位争夺同一批租客，导致供过于求；从一开始购买价格相对于可实现租金就已过高；或者所在位置根本无法吸引强劲的租赁需求。

供应过剩是个无声杀手。一条走廊在短期内相继推出四五个大型condo项目，业主之间相互竞争就会压低租金，而购买价格却依然居高不下。如果再加上项目距离MRT站或主要就业中心步行不便，那就是租金回报率勉强覆盖成本的典型组合。

对任何有意以租金收入为目的购买condo的人来说，实际的建议是在决定之前就算清楚数字，而不是之后。以该单位类型和地点的实际月租金为基准，乘以12，再除以包含印花税在内的总购买成本。如果毛回报率低于2.5%，数字已经很薄，任何空租期或维修费用都会蚕食所剩无几的收益。了解什么因素会拖低回报率，能给你提供一份在签字前逐一核对的清单。
