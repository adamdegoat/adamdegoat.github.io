The big trap

Buy first, and you pay ABSD upfront

If you buy the new home before selling the old one, you'll have two properties for a moment — so you pay Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (a large sum) upfront, and only claim it back if you sell the first within the timeline. Selling first, or sequencing it right, avoids tying up that cash.

Watch the clock

Are you selling too early?

Sell a private home within 3 years of buying and you pay Seller's Stamp Duty — a penalty that shrinks each year. HDB flats don't have this, but they do have the 5-year occupation rule instead.

1

The three kinds of home

HDB — public, subsidised, with rules on who can buy. Executive Condo (EC) — a hybrid that starts with HDB-style rules and turns fully private after 10 years. Private condo / landed — open market, anyone can buy (foreigners pay heavy extra duty).

2

Lease vs freehold

Most homes here sit on a 99-year lease — a clock that matters, because the home's value and how much loan/CPF you can use both shrink as the lease runs down ("lease decay"). Freehold never expires.

3

Why all the rules?

The stamp duties and loan limits — the "cooling measures" — exist to keep prices in check and stop people over-borrowing. They're the reason your budget is smaller than your bank balance suggests.

For everyone

The words you'll keep hearing.

Every term the tools throw at you — translated once, in plain English, with what it actually means for you.

TDSR — the loan cap

Banks won't let all your monthly repayments add up to more than 55% of your income.More income or less debt → bigger budget.

MSR — HDB & EC only

A tighter cap: your home loan alone can't exceed 30% of your income.Why HDB budgets feel smaller than condo ones.

LTV — how much they lend

The most a bank will lend — usually 75% of the price on your first loan. You find the other 25%.Drops to 55% if the loan runs past age 65 or 30 years — and lower still (around 45%) on a second home loan.

Stress test

Banks check you could still pay if rates jumped to about 4%, even though you'll likely pay less.It's why the "safe" loan feels conservative — that's the point.

BSD — Buyer's Stamp Duty

A tax everyone pays on the purchase price, roughly 1–6% depending on price.Budget for it in cash, on top of the down payment.

ABSD — Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty

Extra duty if it's not your first home, or you're a PR/foreigner. Can be very large.The single biggest cost surprise when upgrading.

SSD — Seller's Stamp Duty

A penalty for selling a private home within 3 years of buying it.Hold past 3 years and it's zero.

CPF + accrued interest

You can use CPF for a home, but on selling you must put back what you used plus the interest it would have earned.It quietly eats into your cash proceeds.

Valuation gap

A bank only lends, and CPF only applies, up to its own valuation of the home.Pay above valuation and the difference is cash on top.

Minimum cash

At least 5% of the price must be paid in actual cash — CPF can't cover this part.The first real cash hurdle to clear.

MOP — Minimum Occupation Period

Usually 5 years you must live in an HDB before you can sell it or buy private.Sets the earliest you can upgrade.

Lease decay

A 99-year home slowly loses value — and loan/CPF eligibility — as the lease counts down.Matters most on older flats and for resale.
