Market Pulse

Condo prices are climbing. The heartland is split. Here's where the market's actually moving.

20 June 2026 · 1-min read · by Adam Phang
→ HDB resale steady▲ Condos rising▼ City centre cooling▲ Clementi +3.9%▲ Bukit Merah +3.2%▼ Geylang -5.9%

Condo prices have been rising over the past few months and the trend is still going. HDB resale is a quieter story. Prices there have been flat for three months straight, steady but not moving much. What makes this week interesting is that the priciest central areas are actually cooling, down around 2.4%, while the suburbs are holding up better. This is not one market doing one thing. It is a few different stories depending on exactly where you look.

Same island, very different markets right now.

If you are looking at condos, the central areas are worth a closer look right now. Prices are easing there, which means you have more room to negotiate than you would have had a few months back. The suburbs are steadier, so sellers there are less likely to move on price. For HDB buyers, the town you are targeting really changes the picture. Clementi has moved up 3.9% and Bukit Merah is up 3.2%, so you are paying noticeably more than before in those areas. Geylang is a different story altogether. Prices there have dropped 5.9%. If the location works for you and your family, there is real value to find right now.

If you are selling a condo in the central areas, price it carefully and honestly. The market there is cooling and buyers can see the same numbers you can. In the suburbs you are in better shape, demand is holding up and you have more pricing power to work with. For HDB sellers, your specific town matters far more than any headline figure. Clementi and Bukit Merah sellers are seeing genuine demand, with prices moving in your favour. If you are in Geylang, you will need to price sharper to get something done. The takeaway is simple: the ones who sell well this month are the ones who understand their street, not just the market in general.
