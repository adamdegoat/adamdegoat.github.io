The catch with that cheaper new flat in Toa Payoh or Clementi

Since late 2024, every new HDB flat is classified as Standard, Plus, or Prime. If you're looking at a new flat in Toa Payoh, Clementi, or Queenstown, it's almost certainly a Plus or Prime — and three conditions come with it that are worth understanding before you apply.

Standard flats work like the old system: live there for 5 years, then sell when you like, rent the whole place out if you want, and keep every dollar of profit.

Plus flats are in well-connected areas; Prime flats are in the most central spots. Both get a bigger upfront discount — and three long-term trade-offs: a 10-year minimum stay before you can sell (double the Standard requirement), a permanent ban on renting the whole flat, and a clawback where HDB takes back a percentage of your sale price.

That clawback ranges from about 6% to 9% of the sale price depending on the project — not a fixed dollar sum, but a slice of whatever the flat fetches when you sell.

On a flat sold for $700,000, that's $42,000 to $63,000 back to HDB before you pocket the rest. You can still rent out spare bedrooms — just not the whole flat.

A Plus or Prime flat can still be a great home. Just know you're signing up for a decade-long commitment and sharing a slice of your eventual gain with HDB.
