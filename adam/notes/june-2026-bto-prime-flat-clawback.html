That Subsidised Bishan Flat Has a String Attached

Almost 7,000 new public housing flats went on sale this week across Bishan, Bukit Merah, Ang Mo Kio, Sembawang, and Woodlands — and the application window closes this Wednesday, 24 June at 11:59pm.

The project I'm getting the most questions about is Lakeview Cascadia in Bishan. Four-room flats start from $534,000 — a price you won't find anywhere on the open market for a new flat this central. Bukit Merah's Berlayar Rise is similar. Both are classified as Prime flats, which means generous subsidies upfront and two firm conditions attached.

First: you can't sell for 10 years, not the usual five. Second: when you do sell, you hand back a percentage of whatever your buyer pays you.

That second part catches people off guard. It's called a subsidy recovery — 10% of your resale price for Bishan, 14% for Bukit Merah. Sell your Bishan flat in 2037 for $900,000 and $90,000 goes back to HDB. The same sale price in Bukit Merah means returning $126,000.

The Sembawang and Woodlands projects this round carry no clawback and no extended lock-in. Some Sembawang units are ready in under three years.

A lower entry price isn't always the cheaper option over time — know your category before you apply.
