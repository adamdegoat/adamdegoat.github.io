Your property budget has three parts. Most buyers only count one.

I've watched buyers spend weeks falling for homes they couldn't afford — because they opened listings before they ran three numbers that every search should start with.

Your budget isn't just your savings. It's your savings plus whatever a bank will lend you — and that loan is capped by a government rule that looks at your total monthly debt load. Every existing loan and credit card you carry eats directly into how much you can borrow for a home. Clear that debt first, and your borrowing power grows.

The down payment itself has two pockets: cash and your CPF Ordinary Account savings — the savings most working Singaporeans build up through mandatory monthly contributions. For a bank loan, part of the down payment must come specifically in cash. Your CPF can cover the rest, but it can't cover everything. If your liquid savings are thin, this is the number that changes things.

Stamp duty and legal fees come from your savings — not from the loan.

Stamp duty is a government charge calculated on the higher of the purchase price or the property's market value, and it adds up quickly. Add legal fees and a realistic renovation budget, and the true funds you need at the start are significantly more than just the down payment figure alone.

Run all three calculations — loan limit, down payment split, and upfront costs — before you look at a single listing.
