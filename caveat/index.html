How to use Caveat

Three tools, one source of truth — official Singapore transaction data. Here's the quick tour.

  1. 1
    Valuation — price a home in seconds.

    Pick HDB or Private, fill in the unit (town/flat-type or project, size, floor, lease) and hit Generate. You'll get an estimate range, the comparable sales it used, a price trend, nearby amenities and a written summary — ready to print or share. Add the street to include amenities.

  2. 2
    Affordability — qualify a buyer.

    Enter the buyer's profile, price and income. Caveat works out ABSD, stamp duty, cash needed upfront, the maximum loan and whether it passes the TDSR/MSR loan limits — the conversation you'd normally need a banker for.

  3. 3
    Prospecting — find leads in your farm area.

    Choose a town or district to see what just sold there, then tap Draft outreach on any unit for a ready-to-send message you can copy into WhatsApp.

What Caveat covers — and what it doesn't

  • ✓ HDB resale flats & private condo/apartment resale (the bulk of the market)
  • ✕ Landed homes & brand-new launches — these price differently and aren't covered yet
  • ✕ It's an indicative estimate from recent comparables, not a bank or official valuation
  • ✓ Data is official (URA, HDB, OneMap) and refreshed regularly — current as shown in the footer
